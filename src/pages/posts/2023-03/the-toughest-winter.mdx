<Abstract>

As an aftershock of the lockdown and deconfinement agony, I started having anxiety attacks every now and then. :scream:

This is the story of my descent into the irrational fear of doing random things. And how I picked up a racket and a pair of drum sticks and replaced cigarettes and procrastination with friends and commitments.

</Abstract>

If you ever suffered from recurring panic attacks you know these can happen in the most random places or situations. A street market, a cab ride, a dinner with some friends, a supermarket isle, a work meeting.

Between the summers of 2020 and 2022 I had many of those moments of fright sneak in on me out of nowhere. Some times just leaving the house for groceries was enough to trigger the scare.

I can see from the desk where I am writing this the spot in the patio where I froze so many times. Where I felt dizzy :dizzy:, nauseated, or trembling. Where my palms started sweating, :sweat: my heart racing, my chest compressed, my breath racing.

Where I looked at sky, took a couple of deep breaths, trying to get a hold of me, trying to walk, trying to think.

> Oh no! Fuck! Not this shit again.

Two full years after it all started, I was pretty much expecting a panic attack any given day. Some were mild and I could get back in control with a glass of water and a few minutes chilling.

But on 3 occasions, if I recall correctly, I ended up in the hospital asking for help.

> Yes I know it's probably all in my mind, but can you please check my chest? it hurts and my brain is telling me that I am about to die at any moment.

## Some things had to change

My last _big_ panic attack happened in May 2022. It started after lunch on a normal Tuesday and it went on for 3 excruciating hours.

> Just a dude in his mid-life crisis, with some weird long blue hair, having a panic attack. He will be fine.

Of course! I always empathised with the doctors and nurses at the hospital. They were proportionally concerned with me versus their pandemic priorities. That is to say: zero preoccupied.

But 3 long hyperventilating and palpitating hours can't be any good for anyone's physical health. Not to speak of the mental health, clearly spiraling into a cycle of recurring, eventually worsening _oh fuck, not again_.

At this point I had already tried a few prescriptions. Mostly placebo and natural stuff. But these weren't working and I was not open to consider anxiolytic medication.

The next step was obviously getting professional mental health assistance.

However, as I looked through numbers I could call, specialities I could get help from, people I could ask for advice, I asked myself

> Did I already try everything within my reach to make this stop?

Obviously not.

## Get with the program

My fitness was also at an all time low, and work related stress probably at an all time high. I was still smoking a few cigarettes a day, drinking too much coffee, and having irregular sleep patterns.

Quitting tobacco and reducing coffee, were evident candidates for a disruptive change. But it was also clear that I had to make other adjustments or the experiment wouldn't last long.

Eventually, I could be making it all worst.

Within a day of my last visit to the hospital, I settled with the following resolutions:

- Quit smoking. Effective immediately, forever.
- Only drink coffee in the morning.
- Take a break from work. The upcoming holidays are a serious thing.
- Get fit. Start doing sports daily.
- Get busy.

This last one was particularly important, as I had already [quit smoking a hundred times](https://quoteinvestigator.com/2012/09/19/easy-quit-smoking/) so I know how important it is to have something to do instead of _that_ cigarette. And _that_ means _each_ time I'd crave a cigarette.

I really look forward to writing about the _get fit_ part and how I started playing (obsessing) about tennis. I will probably also write about the _cold turkey_ experience with overcoming a 30 year old addiction.

Instead, I was thinking about making a serious, purpose driven, commitment to making music with other people. And I mean more than a Thursday evening jam with friends that will get cancelled now and then, leaving me anxious, craving, craving, craving... :smoking:
